Semantic HTML

May 1 2019

In the article “The Secrets of Semantic HTML5 for Document Structure — a Guide”, learn so many details about how to build a good website. It is the first time that I learn HTML, and I feel confused sometime because put some tag in my HTML file, but it doesn’t work sometimes. This article let me realize that HTML has its own rules, so I need to follow the rules and organized logically.

My blog consists of blue and light yellow because I want to put some posts which are relative to dogs. I went to the corgi festival last year, and there were so many corgis are yellow. When they run near by the ocean, the scene look like there are only blue and orange in the scene. Therefore, I choose blue and yellow as the colors of my blog. Because the colors that I choose are relative to the dog’s picture, they would help describe the structure of the content.

The look I am trying to achieve is that I will focus on the alinement of the text because I want to have a very organized design for my blog. Additionally, as the article mentioned previously, web design is different from visual design. Thus, for the layout of my blog, I would like it to be as simple as possible.

The most challenging in terms of the visual design is that I don’t know if my design of blog contain enough information for the readers. If I put too much information in a page of web, the reader would feel there is too much text. However, if I don’t put enough information, the reader might not be able to find what they want to read. This will be a challenge when I design in terms of the visual design.
